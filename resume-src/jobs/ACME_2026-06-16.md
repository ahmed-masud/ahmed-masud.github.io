## Cover Letter

Thirty years ago I founded my first company to build security software that
didn't exist yet. Today I hold a US patent in AI system design, have shipped
production systems to the US Department of Defense, and run an engineering
organisation that processes a billion HTTP requests an hour at F5 Networks.
The throughline is the same: I build things that have to work, and I lead the
teams that build them.

The VP Engineering role at Acme interests me because the scope matches what I've
actually done — not a narrowed version of it. At saf.ai I am the engineering
organisation: I set the culture, the hiring bar, the architecture, the technical
standards, and the delivery process simultaneously. At Trustifier I scaled that
across six concurrent product lines and 100+ enterprise clients. I know what it
costs when VP Eng loses touch with the codebase, and I've never let that happen.

What I bring to the executive layer that's harder to hire for: I can walk into
a board meeting and defend an architectural decision, then walk back to a PR review
and catch the subtle correctness bug. I've done both in the same day. The technical
credibility isn't a historical footnote — it's current. My most recent patent was
issued in 2024.

On team building: I hire slowly, set high bars, and invest in the people who clear
them. The best engineers I've worked with stayed for years because the work was
serious and the standards were real. I don't build teams that feel like a family —
I build teams that feel like a pit crew: everyone knows their role, the communication
is tight, and the car gets back on the track.

I'm fully remote from Ottawa, available immediately, and happy to talk through
specifics. Resume and references available at (613) 879-6913 or ahmed.masud@linux.com.
