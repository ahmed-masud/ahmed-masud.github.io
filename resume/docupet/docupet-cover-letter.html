Dear DocuPet Hiring Team,

I build things that have to work — not demos, not prototypes, but production systems under real load with real consequences. The E-Commerce role at DocuPet caught my attention because the job description reads like it was written by engineers who've actually shipped: the section on AI governance, distinguishing deterministic from non-deterministic problem spaces, preventing inappropriate AI use in billing paths — that's not boilerplate. That's hard-won. I've been living that problem.

At saf.ai I hold US Patent 12,045,224 for a system that encodes data blocks as neural network weights and enforces self-verification under adversarial conditions. The core engineering challenge wasn't the AI — it was the governance: where does non-deterministic AI belong in a correctness-critical path, and where does it need to stop? I designed those guardrails from first principles. The same discipline applies to an order pipeline, a payment reconciliation system, or a refund engine. Different domain, identical problem shape.

On the PHP and web stack: I've been writing PHP since version 3. gScarlet — a product I built and sold commercially to NRCan, the CDND, and a major healthcare clinic — was one of the first web UIs for a Linux IPv4/IPv6 firewall, built in C and PHP5 before frameworks existed. At Trustifier I shipped multi-tenant web platforms with complex session management, access control, and transaction integrity serving 100+ enterprise clients. Symfony is the next chapter on that foundation, not a new language. I'll be productive in it quickly and I'll say so honestly rather than pretend otherwise.

What I bring that's harder to hire for: I lead as an engineer first. I read the code, review the architecture, and unblock the team where it actually matters — not in the standup, in the diff. At saf.ai and Trustifier I've run concurrent engineering streams as a hands-on technical founder. I know what it costs when an EM loses touch with the codebase, and I don't intend to let that happen. The AI-first culture you're building — where engineers own and can explain every line regardless of how it was generated — is how I already think about engineering discipline.

I'm fully remote from Ottawa, available immediately, and genuinely excited about what DocuPet is building. Six million pets enter shelters annually. Ten percent come home. A registration and reunification platform that actually works changes that number — and that's the kind of mission I want my engineering work attached to.

I'd welcome the chance to talk. My resume is at ahmed-masud.github.io/resume/docupet/ and I'm reachable at (613) 879-6913 or ahmed.masud@linux.com.
